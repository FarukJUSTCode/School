Enrolling at our school

Students can apply for enrolment at MacGregor State High School under the following categories:

  • Catchment area: student’s principal place of residence is within the defined local catchment area.
  • Non catchment: student’s principal place of residence is outside of the local catchment area however they would like to be considered for enrolment
  • Scholarship Program – Academic, Sporting and Cultural
  • Honours Academic Excellence Program
  • Music Excellence Program
  • Chinese Immersion Excellence Program
  • Performing Arts – Junior Aspiring CAD (JAC) and Centre of Artistic Development (CAD)

Please be advised that our school doesn’t conduct open days, but does offer school tours and information sessions on a regular basis.

If you wish to enrol at MacGregor State High School or enquire about our information sessions, school tours or specialist programs as mentioned above please contact our enrolments officer Arhondi Grespos on 3347 3555 or by email

Application for enrolment is via completion of an Expression of Interest form​. The Expression of Interest form and documentation for our specialist programs are also available from our school office with opening hours of 8:00am to 4:00pm.

Obtaining a Queensland Certificate of Education (QCE)

How to obtain a QCE

The QCE is a school-based qualification awarded to young people who have achieved set standards in a “significant amount of learning” and who have met literacy and numeracy requirements.

A significant amount of learning (20 credit points) includes:

  • minimum 12 credits from completed core
  • remaining 8 credits from a combination of core, preparatory, enrichment and advanced courses of study

A set standard of achievement is:

  • a Sound Level of Achievement (for school subjects)
  • completion (VET studies)

For more information visit www.qcaa.qld.edu.au

Facilities

Canteen

The MacGregor State High School Canteen operates 5 days a week during the school term, closing on the last Wednesday of Term 4 each year.

The Canteen is open to students before school from 8.15am until the first bell and then during each break in the school day, closing at the first bell at the end of the lunch break.

The Canteen has a selection of hot and cold food items daily, as well as cold drinks and ice blocks. We offer a range of options to cover both vegetarian and religious requirements.

Uniform

The Uniform Shop accepts EFTPOS/ Visa / MasterCard and has a layby option available. Telephone credit card payments for items to be collected by students is also possible. This option is not available during January each year and the first two days of each term. The Uniform Shop is manned by only one person during opening hours and if the shop is busy, unfortunately the telephone cannot be answered.

We ask that telephone calls to the shop be restricted to between 9am - 12pm (as our peak time is 8am - 9am) to avoid issues or we ask that you leave a message which will be returned as soon as possible.

Download our current price list and new enrollments information from the right of this page.

If you have any further questions, please call the Uniform Shop on 3349 1775 during standard opening hours.

Library

MacGregor Library is an integral part of learning at our school. We inform, challenge and engage our students through resource selection and explicit instruction across all curriculum areas. A visit to our library will reveal:

  • A range of Information Literacy Tutorials available to students and staff
  • High standards of cataloguing to enable resource discovery
  • Specially selected fiction and non-fiction books, a core of periodicals, daily newspapers, and extensive reference and teacher reference sections
  • Dedicated ICT Help Desk
  • Paid subscriptions to online databases, such as the Online Referencing Generator, and Encyclopedia Britannica.
  • An extensive collection of digital media available through our library OPAC
  • Facilities which include a smart television, photocopier, high quality colour printer and scanner

Parents and Citizens Association

The Parents and Citizens Association (P & C) is a collection of parents, caregivers, school staff and other citizens who wish to promote the education of students at MacGregor State High School.

The scheduled meetings are held in the school's Learning Hub (Library) and commence at 7:30pm. Dates set for 2018 are:

  • 14th February
  • 14th March (Annual General Meeting)
  • 9th May
  • 13th June
  • 8th August
  • 12th September
  • 17th October
  • 28th November

If you are interested in attending, please call our P&C Administrator, Suzette Moore on 3347 3512 or smoor174@eq.edu.au to address any questions or to locate the venue.

Parents and caregivers often think they cannot contribute to their children’s Secondary education. Nothing could be further from the truth as students often do their best when there is a partnership between the student, school and home.

A way of getting involved is through attending P & C meetings or by volunteering in the Canteen. Either way you can provide a tangible benefit to the school.

It is essential to have a functioning P & C presence within any school and while MacGregor is a large school, only a handful of parents keep the Association running.

You only need to commit to a few hours a month to attend meetings and this provides you with the opportunity to voice your views and opinions in a relaxed environment with other parents. I urge anyone interested to attend one of the scheduled meetings to see how the P & C operates.

If this is not your preference, then volunteering in the Canteen also contributes to the school. The activities of the Canteen and Uniform Shop are the sole source of funds which the P & C uses to benefit the school. Please contact our Business Administrator on 3347 3512 to discuss helping out for a few hours, even if it can only be once a term. In exchange, the P & C provides you with a “Thank you” voucher for your child to redeem at the Canteen.
